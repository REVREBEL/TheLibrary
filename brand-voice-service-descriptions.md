# Brand Voice: Service Descriptions

This document contains REVREBEL's service offering copy demonstrating how technical services are positioned with brand voice - balancing expertise, clarity, and personality.

---

## Header: High-Performance Revenue Services

```
HIGH-PERFORMANCE REVENUE SERVICES

REVREBEL'S TACTICAL TOOLKIT FOR TOTAL COMMERCIAL OPTIMIZATION

Your P&L doesn't lie. We get under the hood of your revenue ecosystem — pricing, systems, strategy, demand — and rebuild what's broken, refine what's working, and reveal what's possible.
```

**Voice Notes:**
- Technical precision ("P&L," "revenue ecosystem")
- Automotive metaphor ("under the hood")
- Action-oriented verbs (rebuild, refine, reveal)
- Direct truth-telling ("Your P&L doesn't lie")

## Brand Voice Characteristics in Service Copy

### Tone Attributes:

1. **Technical Credibility**
   - Uses industry acronyms: CRS, PMS, GDS, CPC, ROI
   - Names specific systems: SynXis, Opera
   - Demonstrates depth without over-explaining

2. **Results-Oriented**
   - Every service = tangible outcome
   - Financial impact emphasized
   - Before/after framing

3. **Automotive/Engineering Metaphors**
   - "Under the hood"
   - "Fine-tune"
   - "Distribution engine"
   - "Diagnostic"

4. **Dry Wit & Opinion**
   - "Wishful thinking"
   - "Dusty database"
   - "Make more money" map
   - "Please hold" vs "we'll hold"

5. **Contrarian/Challenger**
   - "Not all bookings are created equal"
   - "Your GDS listing isn't set-and-forget"
   - "Revenue strategy without forecasting is wishful thinking"

6. **Direct About Money**
   - "Less money out the door"
   - "More profitable lanes"
   - "Make more money"
   - No euphemisms

## Service Description Rules

### ✅ DO:

- Lead with insight or contrarian statement
- Use technical terminology appropriate to audience
- Include specific problem types (not vague "challenges")
- End with outcome or value promise
- Use metaphors from tech, automotive, engineering
- Be direct about financial impact

### ❌ DON'T:

- Start with "We help companies..."
- Use buzzwords without substance ("synergistic," "leverage")
- Be vague about methodology
- Over-promise ("guaranteed results")
- Use corporate clichés ("best-in-class," "world-class")
- Shy away from money talk

## Audience Adaptation

### For Technical Buyers (Revenue Managers, BI)
- Use specific system names
- Include technical details
- Reference data/analytics directly

### For Executive Buyers (GMs, Owners)
- Emphasize P&L impact
- Use business outcomes
- Balance detail with clarity

### For Operations (Front Office, Sales)
- Focus on workflow improvements
- Show day-to-day benefits
- Include conversion/efficiency wins
