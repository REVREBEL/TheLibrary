# Brand Voice: Email Templates

This document contains REVREBEL's email templates demonstrating brand voice across various transactional and operational communications.

---

## Signed Agreement Confirmation

### Primary Template: "Racers, Rev Your Engines"

**Subject Line:**
```
RACERS, REV YOUR ENGINES — WE'VE GOT A NEW HOTEL TO OPTIMIZE!
```

**Header:**
```
THRILLING UPDATES AHEAD, $RECIPIENT_NAME$
```

**Body Copy:**
```
The ink is dry, the deal is done, and your hotel is officially on the REVREBEL grid.

We're fired up to get started — and now that we're teammates, here's your fully executed agreement for the archives. You can download it below, save it, frame it, or ceremoniously delete the draft version you've been staring at for a week.

[DOWNLOAD AGREEMENT]

Welcome to REVREBEL. We don't just work with hotels — we work with legends in the making. Let's get to it.

$SENDER_FIRST_NAME$
SENT WITH STRATEGY, FROM REVREBEL
```

**Footer:**
```
[Update Your Preferences]
[Unsubscribe]
```

### Alternative Options

#### Option 1: Short & Sweet (Tech-Forward)

**Subject:** Confirmed: Partnership Initialized

**Body:**
```
Contract received and executed. run: initiate_partnership.exe

The sequence is complete. All systems are green. We're officially in this together.

Our team is already provisioning your project environment and will be in touch shortly to schedule our formal kickoff.

Time to make the competition nervous.
```

**Voice Notes:** Technical metaphor, command-line aesthetic, confidence without fluff

## Meeting Cancellation

**Subject:** PLAN CHANGE: YOUR MEETING JUST GOT THE AXE (POLITELY)

**Body:**
```
Hey $RECIPIENT_NAME$,

Looks like the universe (or a calendar conflict) has stepped in — your meeting with $STAFF_NAME$ originally set for $APPOINTMENT_TIME$ is now officially off the books.

We'll be in touch to reschedule, but in the meantime: take this unexpected free time and do something wildly productive. Or just sit in silence. Both are valid.

Team REVREBEL

SENT FROM A CANCELED CALENDAR HOLD
```

**Voice Notes:**
- Direct and honest about cancellation
- Humor to soften disappointment
- Respects recipient's time
- Casual but professional sign-off

## Meeting Rescheduled

**Subject:** PLOT TWIST: YOUR MEETING HAS MOVED

**Body:**
```
Hi $RECIPIENT_NAME$,

We've done some calendar choreography and your meeting with $STAFF_NAME$ has been rescheduled to:

**New Time:** $NEW_TIME$
**Time Zone:** $TIME_ZONE$

**Link:** $MEETING_LINK$

Same smart people, same great conversation — just a new timestamp. Let us know if it doesn't work and we'll pivot (again).

Team REVREBEL

SENT FROM THE DEPARTMENT OF FLEXIBILITY
```

**Voice Notes:**
- "Plot twist" creates narrative framing
- "Calendar choreography" adds personality
- Clear information hierarchy
- Acknowledges inconvenience with humor
- Offers flexibility

## Brand Voice Characteristics in Email

### Tone Attributes Demonstrated:

1. **Confident, Not Corporate**
   - "We don't just work with hotels — we work with legends in the making"
   - "Time to make the competition nervous"

2. **Technical/Nerdy References**
   - "run: initiate_partnership.exe"
   - "All systems are green"
   - "Provisioning your project environment"

3. **Pop Culture/Gaming Metaphors**
   - "Player Two Has Entered the Game"
   - "Character select screen"
   - "Asteroid field of bad data"

4. **Dry Wit & Humor**
   - "Ceremoniously delete the draft version you've been staring at for a week"
   - "Or just sit in silence. Both are valid"
   - "SENT FROM A CANCELED CALENDAR HOLD"

5. **Direct & Honest**
   - "The ink is dry, the deal is done"
   - "Looks like the universe (or a calendar conflict) has stepped in"

### Signature Elements:

- **Custom sign-offs:** "SENT WITH STRATEGY, FROM REVREBEL" / "SENT FROM THE DEPARTMENT OF FLEXIBILITY"
- **Subject line energy:** ALL CAPS for emphasis, em dashes for drama
- **Action metaphors:** "Rev your engines" / "Plot twist" / "Access granted"
- **Conversational parentheticals:** "(or a calendar conflict)" / "(again)" / "(POLITELY)"

## Usage Guidelines

### When to Use Each Template Style:

**Racing/Energy Template** → High-value milestone moments (signed contracts, launches)
**Tech-Forward Template** → Technical audience, developer-focused clients
**Gaming Template** → Younger brands, startup culture clients
**Sci-Fi Template** → Strategic/analytical positioning, enterprise clients

### Email Voice Rules:

✅ **DO:**
- Use humor to soften transactional communications
- Include cultural references that resonate with tech/startup audience
- Be direct about logistics (dates, times, links)
- Add personality to subject lines
- Acknowledge recipient's time and experience

❌ **DON'T:**
- Use corporate jargon ("per our conversation," "circling back")
- Overuse exclamation points (one per email max)
- Be vague about next steps
- Apologize excessively
- Use passive voice
